What next ...?

My name is Martha Willow and I am an author, professor and historian studying the history of Nova Scotia in the 18th century. In particular, I study the interactions between the First Nation tribes and the settlers that led to a fusion of their knowledge, language and customs.

Over the course of my work I came upon a letter from a man called John Creighton who, along with four other men, founded a village called Lunenburg in 1753.

In 1782, Lunenburg was subjected to an attack from an American privateer, an event called the Sack of Lunenburg. You can read more about this historical event on another section of this website, but this isn't required for you to suceed in your mission.

During this attack, John Creighton was made prisoner and taken to Boston (USA) while his captors waited for a ransom to be paid. It is not clear what happened, but no ransom was ever paid and shortly after his arrival, John Creighton was sent back home to Lunenburg.

Not a very mysterious story ... until a few weeks ago when I found a letter from John Creighton along with two maps, folded in the spine of an old Bible abandoned in a barn that was being demolished. The letter was written immediately upon his return to Lunenburg in March 1782 and describes a mysterious and powerful 'artefact' that he acquired after being captured and hid upon his return to Lunenburg.

The letter gives clues to the location of the artefact and I immediately stopped all my regular work to look for this location, following the directions he left in his letter:

  • Find what he calls the 'primary location'
  • Find and decrypt hidden instructions to go from the 'primary location' to the final location
  • Locate and retrieve the artefact
