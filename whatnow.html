The discovery

A few weeks ago I found an old envelope tightly folded in the spine of an old Bible under the eaves of a barn that was being demolished near a village called Lunenburg, on the South Shore of Nova Scotia.

The envelope contained a letter and two maps, hidden there by one of the founders of Lunenburg, a man called John Creighton who was taken captive during the Sack of Lunenburg in July 1782. After the fight, they were taken to Boston where they were held hostage for ransom by the American invaders. There is no record of a ransom being paid but a few weeks after their arrival they were sent back to Lunenburg.

It appears that at some point, either during their imprisonment in Boston or on the ship on their way back, they met a man who was likely some kind of adventurer/mercenary. There is no record of the man's name or where he was from, but there is evidence that he gave John Creighton a pouch containing three sacred and powerful objects which the man called 'sacratium' and claimed would grant magical powers to those who knew how to work the ritual.

John Creighton appears to have been convinced of the sacratium's powers and upon arrival in Lunenburg, afraid of its powers, prompty buried it to keep it out of the hands of those who might misuse it.

I have included the letter in your package. I will let you read his letter now, it will give you more information.



The search begins

I can't resist an old map and the letter was so intriguing that I immediately started working on the clues to locate the sacratium. Soon it was taking all my time so I put my own research on hold and called this new project: Lunenburg Lost.

It took me a couple of months and a lot of reading and researching and rubbing shoulders with more crusty, dusty archive keepers that I care to say, but I managed to work out the first clue in John Creighton's letter: I found the location of the houses of his four children's godfather, figured out how to pair them, and from then it was easy to mark the X at their center.

Shortly after, I was able to work out the second clue in the letter: I found and decrypted the hidden instructions John Creighton hid on the map of Lunenburg...

It was so exciting, I was getting so close!



Danger looms

Just as I was on the verge of starting on the final step and use both maps to find the location of the sacratium, I received a very generous offer from a large multinational bank to buy John Creighton's letter and maps and desist from any further research. I was so careful not to mention my research to anyone, I couldn't figure out how they would have even heard of me. Being naturally cautious I turned down their offer, at least until I found the sacratium and understood what it was I had in my possession.

They increased their already considerable offer, and that made me downright suspicious. And then a few days later, a colleague whose research I knew they were funding was seen trying to break into my office after I had left for the day. That same night, I had the impression I might be followed ... just shadows in the dark and quiet footsteps behind me, so I can't be sure but I was getting increasingly concerned for my safety.

Then one day last week, while most of the staff was out for lunch, a fire broke out in my office building and the alarm didn't go off because it has been tampered with. I barely had time to run back to my office and throw the contents of my desk in a bag before running outside to safety. I don't think the fire was an accident. I knew I wasn't safe anymore and that it wouldn't be long before they tried again.

In order to keep myself and John Creighton's documents safe, I devised a plan. I would leave Lunenburg for a while, hoping 'they' would follow me thinking I would take them to the location of the buried sacratium. That would leave the coast clear for someone else to continue the quest, someone who they would not be aware of.

And this is where you come in ...
